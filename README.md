# Hi there 👋

I'm **Erkmen G. Aslim**, an Assistant Professor of Economics at the University of Vermont. I'm also a Research Affiliate at IZA@LISER.

## 🔬 Research Areas
- Applied Microeconomics  
- Health Economics  

## 👨‍🔬 About Me
My research examines how public policies and economic shocks shape **health, labor markets, and social outcomes**. I focus particularly on how institutions and policy environments affect vulnerable populations, including **immigrants, justice-involved individuals, low-income families, and children**.

Using large-scale administrative and survey data together with modern causal inference methods, my work studies how policies influence **economic behavior, wellbeing, and social outcomes** across a range of contexts.

## 📊 Current Research Themes
Much of my research explores how policy changes ripple through economic and social systems. Current and ongoing work examines topics such as:

- 🏥 How health policy and medical innovations influence labor markets and economic wellbeing  
- ⚖️ The intersection of public policy, economic hardship, and criminal behavior  
- 👶 How reproductive policy shapes family outcomes and child wellbeing  
- 🤖 The economic and social consequences of new technologies and innovations in healthcare
